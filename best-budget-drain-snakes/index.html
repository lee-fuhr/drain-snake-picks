A slow or clogged drain is one of those annoyances that can quickly escalate into an expensive plumber call. Budget drain snakes let you tackle the problem yourself for a fraction of the cost, but the right tool depends on where the clog lives and how often you deal with it. Before you buy, think about the clog depth, your pipe diameter, and how often you’ll use the tool. Manual augers are fine for occasional sink or shower clogs up to 25 feet, while electric models handle tougher jobs and longer runs. Hair clogs respond best to flexible barbed tools, not aggressive cables. Material quality and ease of retracting the cable also matter – a cheap, stiff cable can be more hassle than help.
